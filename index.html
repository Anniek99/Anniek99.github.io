Annie Katz — Hands-On Engineering Portfolio

I build biomedical engineering devices inspired by real problems my family, friends, and I experience. My projects combine sensors, wearables, mechanical design, and embedded systems to help people move, heal, and sense their bodies better.

Individual Projects

GI Tracked — Wearable Gut Rhythm Monitoring System

Wearables Biosignals Sensors

Problem: No tool exists to track gut motility during daily life, despite its importance in health.

Build: A multi-sensor abdominal band with contact microphones + thermistors and an ESP32-S3 logger.

Challenges: Motion noise, placement reproducibility, long-wear comfort.

Impact: Potential for early detection of gut dysregulation and circadian rhythm misalignment.

Imaging Rehabilitation

At-Home Muscle Ultrasound

Problem: I re-tore my hamstring because I couldn’t monitor healing between clinic visits.

Build: Low-cost ultrasound probe + image-processing pipeline to view muscle recovery.

Impact: Could help young athletes avoid reinjury.

Family-Driven Engineering Projects

Rehabilitation Motor Control

Grip Hero (v1)

Problem: My sister with dyspraxia struggled with existing grip tools.

Build: Load-cell based trainer with simple visual biofeedback.

Learning: First realization that mechanical friction ruins sensor accuracy.

Iteration Human-centered design

Gripster (v2)

Focus: Smoother rails, ergonomic handle, clearer force feedback.

Goal: Make therapy intuitive + motivating for young users.

Result: Reliable, low-friction motion and stable load cell data.
