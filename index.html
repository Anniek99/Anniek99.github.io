Annie Katz — Hands-On Engineering Portfolio

I build biomedical engineering devices inspired by real problems my family, friends, and I experience. My projects combine sensors, wearables, mechanical design, and embedded systems to help people move, heal, and sense their bodies better.

Individual Projects

GI Tracked — Wearable Gut Rhythm Monitoring System

A reusable abdominal band that records gut sounds and temperature overnight using contact microphones, an NTC thermistor, and a QT Py ESP32-S3. Built to study digestive rhythms, sleep patterns, and circadian alignment at home.

At-Home Muscle Ultrasound System

After re-tearing my hamstring by returning to hockey too early, I built a low-cost ultrasound prototype to visualize tendon and muscle healing between clinic visits.

Scandage — Home Diagnostic Screener

A sensor + software prototype that performs early screening to reduce diagnostic delays. Designed to give families a way to identify concerning patterns before symptoms escalate.

Family-Driven Engineering Projects

Grip Hero (v1)

A load-cell based hand-strength trainer created for my sister with dyspraxia. This version taught me how mechanical friction destroys sensor accuracy.

Gripster (v2)

Full mechanical redesign: smoother rails, ergonomic handle, reduced friction, stable force readings, and motivating visual feedback.

Misophonia Sensory Aid (Work in Progress)

A wearable sound-filtering device to help my younger brother manage sensitivity to sudden triggering frequencies.

Team Projects

HeadHush — Smart Therapy Beanie

I built the conductive textile layer and integrated heat/vibration modules into this migraine-relief beanie controlled through a mobile interface.

Pressure-Sensing Cast (Work in Progress)

Designed a cast liner with embedded pressure sensors to detect pressure sores early— especially important because both my brothers ended up in casts this year.
